## Detailed Notes

- M28 classifies systems as `NO`, `INF rank`, or `UNIQUE ...`; fixed and stress
  tests exercise row swaps, inconsistent zero rows, free variables, and modular
  inverse normalization.
- M29 computes `A^e * v` with column-vector semantics; tests include `e = 0`,
  non-symmetric matrices, triangular powers, and a large exponent.
- M30 infers the minimal recurrence from a prefix with Berlekamp-Massey and
  evaluates distant terms with polynomial reduction; tests include prefix
  returns, signed coefficients modulo `MOD`, all-zero recurrence, and constant
  sequence degeneracy.
- M31 computes modular determinants with row-swap sign handling; M32 applies
  Gauss-Jordan inverse detection; M33 builds a raw 60-bit XOR basis. The M33
  stress oracle initially reused a modular vector printer, truncating 60-bit
  inputs; targeted stress caught it and the generator now writes raw XOR values.
- M34 counts weighted undirected spanning trees by deleting an arbitrary
  cofactor from the Laplacian. M35 counts rooted in-arborescences by deleting the
  root from an in-degree directed Laplacian. M36 applies the fixed-first-edge
  BEST theorem variant: directed arborescences rooted at the fixed start times
  `prod_v (outdeg(v)-1)!`, after Eulerian degree checks.
- M37 outputs `det(xI-A)` coefficients from high to low; stress catches sign and
  ordering errors. M38 validates trace powers including `t=0` and non-diagonal
  matrices. M39 answers independent `det(A+uv^T)` queries and catches both
  `u^T A^{-1} v` and cumulative-update mistakes.
